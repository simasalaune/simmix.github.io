3d art

blender

FIRST RENDERS

After tackling the most famous Blender donut tutorial, I decided to spice things up with my own style. My 1st steps in the 3D Art realm started with an animal-crossing inspired rabbit buddy and a bioluminescent mushroom biome in a bottle. Not too shabby for a 3D rookie, ey?

BOXX-E

My first 3D hurdle came in a university class where I got to whip up a 1-minute animation from scratch – main character, scenario, and backgrounds, you name it! Picture this: a futuristic sci-fi robot helper on a mission to move boxes across a spaceship. I crafted a large portion of the overall project – from the cute little box robot to the vibrant environments and animations (except for the hand-robot, that was my groupmate’s part). Thrilled with how this 3D animation turned out in the end!

star guardian sword

Summer of 2023 rolled in, and guess what? I was back at it, leveling up my 3D Art skills with yet another YouTube tutorial. Of course, as always I put my own spin on it! This time, I finished CG Fast Track’s sword tutorial and rendered a Star Guardian-inspired sword. Because why not add a touch of cosmic flair to my 3D arsenal?

BOSS CHALLENGE 2023

August 2023, I jumped into pwnisher’s Boss Challenge – a month-long 3D challenge creating a short slow-mo animation with a given template. My personal goal? Showcase the epic size difference between the Boss and the main character. Went for a spooky circus clown theme just for kicks. Didn’t make the final YouTube trailer, but hey, super proud of the final render in the end!

grease pencil halloween

Come Halloween 2023, I set myself a little mission: conquer the art of grease pencil in Blender by whipping up three simple Halloween-themed renders. Thrilled with the spooky results! Ghost-face is definitely my favorite one. 🎃👻
